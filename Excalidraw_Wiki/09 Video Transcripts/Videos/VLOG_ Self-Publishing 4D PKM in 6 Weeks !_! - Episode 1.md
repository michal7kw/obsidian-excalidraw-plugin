# Summary
In this video, the creator outlines an ambitious plan to write and self-publish a book on Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) in just six weeks. The goal is to share the process, learnings, and the final product at the PKM Summit in March. Despite having a full-time job and numerous commitments, the creator aims to leverage various tools and community support to make this project successful.

# Key Takeaways
- The creator plans to write and self-publish a PKM book within six weeks.
- They invite community involvement and feedback to help shape the book's content and structure.
- Emphasis is placed on the use of modern tools to aid in writing, despite the creator's self-identified lack of writing skills.

# Content
I have a crazy plan, and this might turn out to be a major flop. I want to write a book, but I want to write it in six weeks and self-publish it in six weeks so that I can present my process, my learnings, and the book at the PKM Summit in Utrecht, the Netherlands, on 14th and 15th March this year. The title of the book is "4D PKM." My goal is to publish something that's enjoyable to read and informative. Right now, I have nothing; I have a blank sheet of paper. So this might be learning in public or failing in public, but regardless of the outcome, I think I'm going to learn a lot from the process, and I hope that you're going to learn a lot too. Of course, I have notes—lots of notes—in Obsidian: over 177,000 notes, I have lots of drawings, I have YouTube transcripts. Now, I am not going to be doing this full time; I'm running cohort 12 of the Visual Thinking Workshop at the same time, I have a full-time job, so this is just a part-time hobby. As well as, of course, I'm maintaining the Excalidraw plugin, which takes quite some time. So we will see if we can use all the tools today with Notebook LM, with Excal writing, machine with AI for templator, with JGPT, and the many other tools to compensate for the time and for my lack of writing skills because I'm not a writer. I've never been a good writer, but I think tools nowadays should be able to compensate for that. [* ](https://youtu.be/-GIt4LKSRoA?t=0)

What I'm going to do is create this Vlog of me trying to self-publish a book. Also, I'm going to organize regular sessions every couple of days with the members of the Visual PKM Community. You'll find a link in the description below; if you're interested, join the community, join me in these discussions. We're going to be talking about the process, the tools, the technology, and the progress. I'm going to be looking for feedback as well as sharing my learnings along the journey. Of course, if you're just interested in these videos, then I am very happy with that as well, so please follow my Vlog. Again, I'm going to be publishing an update regularly, and if you want, then come to the PKM Summit, and we can meet there as well. [* ](https://youtu.be/-GIt4LKSRoA?t=54)

In the comments below, share your ideas and suggestions. I'm super interested to hear how you would form the concept for the book, what the book should be about, how deep the book should go, what tools I should consider—anything that can help me. I appreciate your comments, so see you in the next video! [* ](https://youtu.be/-GIt4LKSRoA?t=200)
