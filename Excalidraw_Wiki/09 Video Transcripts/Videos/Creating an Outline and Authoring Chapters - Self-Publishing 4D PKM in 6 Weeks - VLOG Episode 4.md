# Summary
In this video, Zsolt provides an update on his progress with his upcoming book about visual PKM, detailing his writing process and the tools he has been using. He shares insights into the structure of the book, which consists of 15 chapters, and highlights the methodologies that have allowed him to generate content efficiently. Zsolt expresses excitement about the book's potential impact and the insights gained from his iterative writing process.

# Key Takeaways
- Zsolt's book outline is 9,000 characters long, dividing the content into four parts with a total of 15 chapters.
- He utilizes multiple tools, including Google’s Gemini and ChatGPT, to assist with generating text and improving his book outline.
- The iterative process of seeking feedback and refining his outline has allowed him to incorporate personal anecdotes and reflections, making the content more meaningful.

This was now based on the initial outline, my feedback on it, and the Notebook LM additions. For improving on this outline, I followed the following process: I took various books from my list of books that I've read and want to use as references in this book project, and I asked Notebook LM to generate a podcast about each of those books, a separate 20-30 minute podcast that focuses on that specific book. As I was listening to that podcast about a specific source, I had ChatGPT in hand and was having a dialogue about what I'm hearing, how it relates to my outline and to my book project. In that process, I actually created this dialogue transcript with ChatGPT that's both a reflection on my book outline as well as a reflection on the source. Then, I asked ChatGPT, I fed my book outline to it, and of course, the dialogue transcript was there, and I asked for feedback on my outline, which I edited in Obsidian. I got a new version of the outline and went through this process, I think five times, with five different books. Each time, I generated a podcast from Notebook LM, listened to it, did the dialogue transcript, and then took the next version of my book outline to ChatGPT. I asked for feedback, updated my outline in Obsidian, and through these cycles, I was able to mold the initial outline that Gemini provided into something that is really personal. That's based on my own personal experience, filled with personal anecdotes and reflections, and it is really something that is mine. So now I have an outline that I started with an initial outline that helped me get from the blank page to something meaningful. Through this process, I achieved two things: I refreshed my memory about a whole bunch of important books on the subject and was able to refine my outline. Now that I have the outline, I went over to ChatGPT and asked it first to condense this 9,000-word long outline into something short because when I'm producing chapters, I need to make sure that everything important fits into the context window, and 9,000 words just for the full outline is very expensive. First of all, I feed that into Notebook LM to ask for chapter or section relevant references. Then, I take those reference sources as well as my chapter outline, the detailed one, and my condensed entire book outline, and I feed it into my ChatGPT project. Before generating my chapter, I ask ChatGPT to ask me clarification questions to have a discussion about that chapter. Once I'm done with that, I provide some of these inputs again, so they're at the top of the context window, and with this input, I ask ChatGPT to generate my section draft. I review it and edit it in ChatGPT, and when I'm comfortable, I save it as my chapter in Obsidian. This way, I feel that this process right here, because I have everything in the previous two steps, I have everything covered.
